Frequently Asked Questions

Open the app and tap "Scan Certificate." Point your camera at a GIA diamond certificate — the app will automatically detect and read the certificate. It works with both QR codes printed on certificates and the text/plot information. For best results, ensure the certificate is well-lit and flat.
DiamondCheck AI currently supports GIA (Gemological Institute of America) diamond grading reports. This includes both full reports and dossiers. Support for other grading laboratories may be added in future updates.
Each detailed diamond report costs one credit. You receive a free credit when you first install the app. Additional credits can be purchased in-app through the App Store. Credits are linked to your device and do not expire.
Our pricing is based on current market data from thousands of comparable diamonds. Estimates reflect wholesale market pricing and should be used as a reference point. Actual retail prices vary by vendor, and factors like fluorescence, location, and market conditions can affect the final price. Always consult a professional appraiser for high-value transactions.
GIA certificates include a clarity plot — a diagram showing the location and type of inclusions in the diamond. Our AI analyzes this plot to assess how inclusions affect the stone's appearance and value. This helps you understand the difference between, say, two SI1 diamonds where one may be "eye-clean" and the other has visible inclusions. The pricing difference between a good SI1 and a fair SI1 can be 30% or more — clarity plot analysis helps you spot that.
Credits are linked to your device. If you reinstall the app on the same device, your credits will be preserved. If you switch to a new device, use the "Restore Purchases" option in the app to recover any purchased credits through the App Store.
Make sure the certificate is well-lit with even lighting (avoid glare and shadows). Hold the phone steady and ensure the full certificate is visible in the frame. If the paper certificate is difficult to scan, you can also try scanning the digital certificate from the GIA website on a screen. If problems persist, contact us at the email below.
