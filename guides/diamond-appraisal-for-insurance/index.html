Diamond Appraisal for Insurance

What insurers require, how valuations work, and how to keep your diamond coverage current.

If you own a diamond — whether it's an engagement ring, a loose stone, or a family heirloom — getting a proper diamond appraisal for insurance is essential. Without one, you could be underinsured or unable to file a claim if your diamond is lost, stolen, or damaged.

Why You Need a Diamond Appraisal for Insurance

Most homeowner's and renter's insurance policies have a limit on jewelry coverage — typically $1,000-$2,500. If your diamond is worth more than that (and most engagement rings are), you'll need a separate jewelry insurance policy or a rider on your existing policy.

To get that coverage, your insurer will require a jewelry appraisal — a documented assessment of the diamond's characteristics, quality, and replacement value.

What an Insurance Appraisal Includes

A proper diamond appraisal for insurance should document:

  • Diamond specifications — carat weight, cut, color, clarity, shape, and measurements
  • Replacement value — the current retail cost to replace the diamond with one of equivalent quality
  • Certification reference — the GIA or other grading report number
  • Setting details — metal type, style, and condition of the mounting
  • Date of appraisal — insurers require a recent valuation

Insurance Value vs. Resale Value

One of the biggest sources of confusion is the difference between insurance value and resale value:

Insurance value (replacement value) is what it would cost to replace your diamond at current retail prices. This is typically the highest valuation and is what your insurance policy is based on.

Resale value is what you'd receive if you sold the diamond on the secondary market. This is typically 25-50% of retail, reflecting the wholesale-to-retail markup.

Market value is the wholesale or fair market price based on current trading data (like Rapaport prices). This falls between insurance and resale value.

Understanding all three gives you the full picture of what your diamond is worth in different contexts.

How Often to Get Reappraised

Diamond prices change over time. Insurance experts recommend getting your diamond reappraised every 2-3 years to ensure your coverage matches current replacement costs.

An outdated appraisal can cause two problems:

  • Underinsured — If diamond prices have risen, your coverage may not be enough to replace your stone
  • Overpaying premiums — If prices have dropped, you could be paying for more coverage than you need

How to Get a Diamond Appraisal for Insurance

You have several options:

  • Certified gemologist — A GIA or AGS certified appraiser provides a formal document accepted by all insurers. Typical cost: $50-$150 per item.
  • Jewelry store — Many jewelers offer appraisals, often free with purchase. Be aware these may use inflated replacement values.

Engagement Ring Appraisal Tips

If you're getting an engagement ring appraisal for insurance, keep these tips in mind:

  • Get the appraisal done within 30 days of purchase for the smoothest insurance process
  • Keep your GIA certificate — it's the most important supporting document
  • Photograph the ring from multiple angles as additional documentation
  • Ask your jeweler if they provide an appraisal at the time of purchase
  • Compare the appraisal value to market data to ensure it's reasonable
