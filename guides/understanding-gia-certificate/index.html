Understanding Your GIA Certificate

A complete guide to reading a GIA diamond grading report — what each section means and how it affects your diamond's value.

A GIA certificate (officially called a GIA Diamond Grading Report) is the most trusted and widely recognized diamond grading document in the world. Whether you're buying, selling, or insuring a diamond, understanding your GIA certificate is essential.

What Is GIA?

The Gemological Institute of America (GIA) is a nonprofit organization founded in 1931 that created the 4 Cs grading system used worldwide. GIA doesn't buy or sell diamonds — they only grade them, which is why their reports are considered the most objective and reliable in the industry.

Sections of a GIA Diamond Grading Report

Every GIA certificate contains the same key sections. Here's what each one means:

Report Number

A unique identification number assigned to the diamond. You can use this number to verify the report on GIA's Report Check service online. For many diamonds, this number is also laser-inscribed on the diamond's girdle (the thin edge around the widest point).

Shape and Cutting Style

Describes the diamond's shape (Round Brilliant, Princess, Oval, Cushion, etc.) and how it was cut. Round Brilliant is the most popular and typically commands the highest per-carat prices.

Carat Weight

The diamond's weight measured to the hundredth of a carat. One carat equals 0.2 grams. Price per carat increases significantly at key thresholds — a 1.00ct diamond is worth considerably more per carat than a 0.99ct diamond of equal quality.

Color Grade

Graded on a scale from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown):

  • D-F — Colorless. The most rare and valuable.
  • G-J — Near-colorless. Excellent value, as color is difficult to detect face-up.
  • K-M — Faint color. Slightly visible warmth.
  • N-Z — Noticeable color. Significantly lower value.

Clarity Grade

Evaluates the presence of inclusions (internal) and blemishes (external):

  • FL / IF — Flawless / Internally Flawless. Extremely rare.
  • VVS1 / VVS2 — Very, Very Slightly Included. Inclusions are difficult to see under 10x magnification.
  • VS1 / VS2 — Very Slightly Included. Minor inclusions visible under magnification.
  • SI1 / SI2 — Slightly Included. Noticeable inclusions under magnification, sometimes visible to the naked eye.
  • I1 / I2 / I3 — Included. Visible inclusions that may affect brilliance.

Cut Grade (Round Brilliants Only)

GIA assigns a cut grade only to round brilliant diamonds, ranging from Excellent to Poor. The cut grade evaluates brightness (white light reflection), fire (spectral colors), and scintillation (sparkle pattern).

Cut is often considered the most important factor for a diamond's visual beauty. An Excellent cut diamond will outperform a higher color or clarity stone with a lesser cut.

Proportions Diagram

A cross-section diagram showing the diamond's exact measurements:

  • Table % — The flat top facet, as a percentage of the diamond's width
  • Depth % — Total height relative to width
  • Crown angle — Angle of the upper facets
  • Pavilion angle — Angle of the lower facets
  • Girdle thickness — The edge width (thin to thick)
  • Culet — The bottom point (ideally None or Very Small)

These measurements determine how light travels through the diamond and directly affect its brilliance and fire.

Clarity Plot

The clarity plot is a diagram showing the exact location and type of inclusions and blemishes in the diamond. Red symbols indicate internal inclusions (crystals, feathers, clouds, etc.), while green symbols indicate surface blemishes (scratches, nicks, etc.).

Understanding the clarity plot helps you assess whether inclusions are in visible positions (like under the table facet) or hidden near the edges where they're less noticeable.

Fluorescence

Measures the diamond's reaction to long-wave UV light, graded from None to Very Strong. Strong or Very Strong fluorescence in higher-color diamonds (D-H) can make the stone appear hazy or milky, reducing value by 10-15%. In lower-color diamonds (I+), medium fluorescence can actually make the stone appear whiter and may add value.

How to Verify Your GIA Certificate

Always verify a GIA certificate before making a purchase:

  • Visit GIA's official Report Check page online
  • Enter the report number from the certificate
  • Compare the results with the physical certificate
  • If the diamond has a laser inscription, check that the girdle inscription matches the report number
